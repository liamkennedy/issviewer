About

ISS Viewer is a free app that makes spotting the International Space Station very easy. It predicts ISS passes and displays them on a map. All you have to do is look at the sky at the right time to see it! The source code is available on Github. It was created, because of a lack of beginner friendly web apps for spotting the ISS.

ISS is so far away, so why it is visible from Earth? It's a big object and it reflects a lot of light. So much light that it can be a lot brighter than any star on the night sky. That's why you don't need a telescope to see it.

Learn more about ISS on wikipedia

ISS Viewer uses TLE data to calculate ISS position and predict passes.
